An explosion rocked the Royal Marines School of Music in a southeastern coastal town today, causing one building to collapse and killing eight people, officials said.
The Irish Republican Army claimed reponsibilty for the explosion, which police said killed 11 people and injured 22.
Stunned neighbors gathered outside homes that were damaged or destroyed.
A huge explosion in a barracks staff room Friday morning leveled the three-story building as bandsmen took a coffee break between practice sessions on the school's parade ground.
Residents of the area adjoining the Marines school said Friday that despite government calls for stepped-up public alertness against terrorists, security at the school had eased noticeably.
Jean Cleatheroe, whose son Andrew was killed, joined local residents and opposition politicians in denouncing the use of private security firms to protect the music school and 29 other military establishments in Britain.
Defense Secretary Tom King said Sunday he has issued an alert to all military installations to prevent other attacks.
